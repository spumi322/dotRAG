*Basic principles of database security, knowledge of roles, permissions, and access management.*

_1. Roles:_ Roles are like groups that bundle database users. They help manage permissions efficiently. There are two types of roles: fixed roles that are predefined in the database, and user-defined roles that you can create.

_2. Permissions_: Permissions define what actions a role or user can perform on a database object. They can be granted, denied, or revoked.

_3. Access Management_: This involves controlling who can access the database, what they can access, and what actions they can perform. It often includes authentication (verifying who the user is) and authorization (determining what the user can do).
